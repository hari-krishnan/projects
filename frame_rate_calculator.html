HSDI Frame Rate & Pixel Excursion Calculator

Derived from: Optimal Sampling and Noise Mitigation for Vocal Fold Kinematics in HSDI (under review, Journal of Voice)

Faster cameras don't always mean better data. For periodic motion like vocal fold vibration, there is a bounded feasible interval of frame rates — too slow misses peaks and transitions, but too fast causes inter-frame displacement to fall below one pixel, collapsing velocity SNR by ~6 dB per doubling of frame rate. This tool computes that interval from your imaging parameters, and checks whether your pixel excursion is sufficient for any valid frame rate to exist. Validated across 67 subjects at 4000–16000 fps; Kalman smoothing + sinc interpolation reduced kinematic MAPE by 50–70%.

K_min is the minimum glottal excursion (pixels) needed so that each frame captures ≥ 1 pixel of motion — below this the velocity SNR becomes dominated by quantization noise.
Equation
K_min = fₛ·o·max(s,1) / (f₀·(1+s))

Interpretation
At exactly K = K_min the mean inter-frame displacement δ = 1 px.
Use higher magnification or a lower frame rate if K < K_min.
